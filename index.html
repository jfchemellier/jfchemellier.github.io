Many erroneous literal translations, often produced by low-proficiency bilinguals, can be attributed to a tendency to favor typical translation equivalents; however, the underlying neural mechanism remains poorly understood.

This study investigated this typicality effect in real-time translation with ERP. Compared to typical equivalents, atypical ones elicited an N400 and an f-PNP in congruent trials. Full data on Figshare.
